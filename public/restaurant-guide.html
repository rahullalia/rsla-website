Bring Back the Ghosts

Your old customers forgot about you. Let's fix that.

Before

The Problem

Spice on a Slice had 1,400+ customers in their POS system. Emails and phone numbers from years of orders.

They'd never emailed them. Not once.

These people already knew the food was good. They just... forgot.

What We Did

We pulled everyone who hadn't ordered in 90+ days and sent a simple 3-email sequence:

EMAIL 1: "Did we do something wrong?"
Subject: Did we do something wrong? Hey [First Name], We noticed you haven't been in since [Month]. If we messed up your order, I'd genuinely love to know so I can make it right. If life just got busy, here's 15% off your next pie — no strings attached. Either way, we miss seeing you. — Sal Spice on a Slice
4
EMAIL 2 (4 days later): "Still thinking about you"
Subject: Your pizza's getting cold (just kidding) Hey [First Name], Just a quick follow-up. That 15% off is still waiting for you. Click here to order: [LINK] — Sal
EMAIL 3 (7 days later): "Last chance"
Subject: Closing the loop Hey [First Name], Last note from me. The 15% expires Friday. If you're done with us, no hard feelings. But if you want to give us another shot: [ORDER NOW BUTTON] Thanks for reading. — Sal
Why It Works

The "did we mess up?" angle triggers guilt and curiosity. It doesn't feel like marketing. It feels like a real person checking in. That's why open rates hit 45%+ on these campaigns.

What You Can Do Today

  1. Export your customer list from your POS (Toast, Square, Clover all have this)
  2. Filter for customers who haven't ordered in 90+ days
  3. Send Email 1 using Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or even Gmail BCC
  4. Wait 4 days, send Email 2
  5. Wait 3 more days, send Email 3
Time: 2 hours (first time takes longer)
Cost: Free (or ~$20 for Mailchimp)

The Problem

14 reviews in 30 years. Their competitor down the block had 247.

When someone Googles "pizza near me," Google shows the place with more (and better) reviews first. Spice on a Slice was invisible.

What We Did

Three simple changes:

  1. Timing: Texted customers 2 hours after their order. Not immediately, not the next day. Two hours is when they've eaten and they're satisfied.
  2. Direct link: Not "please leave us a review." A direct link that opens Google Reviews with one tap.
  3. Physical reminder: QR code on every receipt and table tent. Customers scan while waiting. Catches them when they're already thinking about the meal.

What We Did

Week 1: Foundation

  • Added 25 high-quality photos (food, interior, staff, kitchen)

  • Filled out every attribute (outdoor seating, delivery, WiFi, etc.)

Weeks 2-8: Consistency

  • Posted 2x per week (specials, behind-the-scenes, customer shoutouts)
  • Added Q&A (and answered our own FAQs)
  • Responded to every review within 24 hours
Photo Naming Hack

Google reads your file names. "IMG_4382.jpg" tells Google nothing. "best-pepperoni-pizza-queens-ny.jpg" tells Google exactly what you serve and where.
